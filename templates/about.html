Discover Albania

A land of ancient history, stunning landscapes, and warm hospitality

Rich Historical Heritage

Albania's history spans over 2,000 years, from ancient Illyrian tribes to Ottoman rule, and finally to independence in 1912. The country has been shaped by various civilizations, each leaving their mark on the culture, architecture, and traditions that you'll discover today.

The medieval hero Gjergj Kastrioti Skanderbeg successfully resisted Ottoman expansion for 25 years, becoming a symbol of Albanian resistance and independence. His legacy lives on in the heart of Tirana, where Skanderbeg Square serves as the city's main gathering place.

The 20th century brought significant changes, including a period of communist rule under Enver Hoxha from 1944 to 1985. This era left Albania isolated from the world but also preserved much of its natural beauty and traditional culture, which visitors can experience today.

Vibrant Culture & Traditions

Albanian culture is a unique blend of ancient Illyrian traditions, Byzantine influences, and Ottoman heritage. The concept of "Besa" (a pledge of honor) is central to Albanian culture, representing trust, loyalty, and hospitality.

Music and dance play vital roles in Albanian culture. Traditional folk music features unique polyphonic singing, recognized by UNESCO as a Masterpiece of Oral and Intangible Heritage of Humanity. The traditional Albanian folk dances are energetic and colorful, often performed during festivals and celebrations.

The Albanian language is one of the oldest in Europe, belonging to its own branch of the Indo-European language family. Despite centuries of foreign rule, Albanians have preserved their language and cultural identity with remarkable resilience.

Modern Albania & Tirana

Today's Albania is a dynamic country undergoing rapid development while preserving its cultural heritage. Tirana, the capital, perfectly embodies this balance between tradition and modernity with its colorful buildings, bustling cafes, and historic landmarks.

The city has been transformed in recent decades, with new parks, renovated buildings, and a thriving arts scene. Young Albanians are embracing entrepreneurship and creativity, making Tirana one of Europe's most exciting emerging destinations.

Albania offers incredible value for travelers, with world-class experiences at a fraction of the cost of other European destinations. From exploring ancient castles to enjoying pristine beaches, Albania provides authentic experiences without the crowds.

Essential Travel Tips

Currency

Albanian Lek (ALL). Euros are widely accepted in tourist areas.

Language

Albanian is the official language. English is spoken in tourist areas, especially by younger people.

Best Time to Visit

May to September for warm weather. October and April offer mild temperatures and fewer crowds.

Getting Around

Taxis and buses are readily available. Walking is the best way to explore the city center.

Tipping

10% is appreciated in restaurants. Round up taxi fares and tip tour guides.

Cultural Etiquette

Hospitality

Albanians are extremely hospitable. Don't be surprised if locals invite you for coffee or offer help.

Greetings

Handshakes are common. Close friends may kiss on both cheeks.

Dress Code

Dress modestly when visiting religious sites. Casual attire is fine elsewhere.

Photography

Ask permission before photographing people. Some military sites prohibit photography.
